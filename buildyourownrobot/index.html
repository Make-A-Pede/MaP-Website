Updates

7/19/2019

  • When mounting the servo horn, the small screws used have been known to break. You can avoid this by only putting the screws in about 1/2 of the way. If the screws do break, you can add superglue to hold the parts in place.
  • If one of your robot's wheels runs backwards when the robot is first turned on, you can swap the black and red wires going into the motor driver that are coming from that motor.
  • To turn on your robot, install all of the batteries then plug in the Arduino. It will immediately start running the program you loaded. To turn off the robot, unplug the Arduino, then remove one of the batteries.
  • This kit does not include the electronic components needed for the robot to be controlled using our app. However, this is a low cost upgrade you can do yourself and we will be posting more details here soon.
